Question #1...What is Bitcoin?

It’s crypto currency, meaning it’s currency that lives online. You can't feel it, you can't touch it, you can't hold it!

Yes
It’s real money but not hard physical money.

Yes
You can convert it to hard currency like USD, AUD and any single currency in the world.

Yes
You can buy real things likes beers, products and even houses with it.

Yes
There are Bitcoin ATM’s popping up all around the globe.

What’s Bitcoin’s advantage?

It’s decentralised! It’s not tied to any one government, country or body of people meaning it’s left to its own devices.

It’s a political safe haven when a country or government landscape could be in uproar. Bitcoin’s value is driven from supply and demand!

What’s it’s disadvantages?

Because it is decentralised, it could all crash tomorrow with no notice and the dream will be over.

Which is why I'm only play with what you can afford to lose!

So...Who keeps an eye on it? And how does it work?

A whole stack of people/computers called miners (who mining for Bitcoin) who collectively record every single bitcoin transaction that happens.

This is stored on something called the block chain. The block chain gets updated in real-time by the minors.

When a transaction happens, the transaction gets announced into the bitcoin eco system in the form of a puzzle/code. The first person/computer to crack the number puzzle/code gets a fee for doing it the fastest. They then update the block chain with the transaction details and the world keeps turning.
